By The Water

Sea-based Spots

Dinagat Islands is best known for its pristine waters and rock formations accessible by local boat tours.

Must Visit

Pangabangan Blue Lagoon

Known for its striking turquoise waters nestled between limestone rock formations. It's an excellent spot for swimming and taking photos against the bright, clean water.

  • Type: Lagoon / Cove
  • Access: Boat Tour Only
Unique Feature

Lake Bababu

A unique body of water offering a mix of fresh and salt water layers. Getting there involves a short, dense hike, making it a good pairing with nearby beach stops.

  • Type: Inland Lake
  • Access: Boat + Short Trek
Relaxation

Bitaog Beach

A simple, undisturbed stretch of white sand with clear waters. It’s perfect for travelers who just want a relaxed day without too much crowds or commercial activity.

  • Type: Beachfront
  • Access: Boat Tour
Sightseeing

Hagakhak Rock Formation

A distinct, jagged limestone formation standing tall in the water. It offers dramatic coastal views often passed by local boat tours on the way to other beaches.

  • Type: Geologic Formation
  • Access: Scenic Boat Pass
Quiet Coast

Duyos Beach

Another excellent option to add to your boat tour route. It offers fine sand and is often emptier than more well-known neighboring island spots.

  • Type: Beachfront
  • Access: Boat Tour
On The Main Island

Land-based Spots

Things you can reach by motorcycle (habal-habal) or local tricycle around the mainland.

Cultural Site

PBMA Shrine

The Philippine Benevolent Missionaries Association (PBMA) shrine is a massive, visually distinct cultural and religious compound in San Jose. It offers a unique look into local history.

Landmark View

Islander's Castle

Often called the 'White Castle' by locals, this imposing structure sits on a hill overlooking San Jose. While you usually can't go inside, it's a popular quick photo stop on the road.
