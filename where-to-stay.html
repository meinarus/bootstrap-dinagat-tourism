Where To Stay

Simple, comfortable accommodations for your island basecamp.

San Jose (Main Hub)

Most travelers base themselves in the capital of San Jose. It offers the most lodging options, dining, and easy access to boat rentals and land tours.

Standard Hotels

There are several standard hotels and inns in San Jose proper. These offer basic amenities like air conditioning, private bathrooms, and sometimes a small in-house basic restaurant.

Homestays & Lodges

For budget-conscious travelers, homestays and backpacker lodges are available. These provide a more local experience, often with shared spaces and very hospitable hosts.

Booking Advice

It is highly recommended to book your stay in advance, especially during the summer months (March-May). Many local lodges can be contacted directly via social media pages.

Alternative Option

Basilisa Town

While San Jose is the primary hub, the neighboring town of Basilisa is occasionally chosen by long-term travelers.

  • Quieter atmosphere than the capital
  • Closer to some key island hopping destinations
  • Extremely limited lodging choices compared to San Jose
